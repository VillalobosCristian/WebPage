Current Research

Fluctuations and Dynamics of Passive Particles in Confined Bacterial Suspensions

My research focuses on understanding the dynamics of passive particles immersed in active bacterial baths, particularly in spherical confinement. By combining experimental techniques and theoretical modeling, I aim to extract the properties of the bacterial bath and its effect on tracer motion, with implications for both fundamental physics and practical applications in soft matter and biophysics.

Analyzing Spatial Correlations in Bacterial Suspensions

This project delves into spatial and temporal correlations between particle pairs within bacterial suspensions. By analyzing these interactions in confined geometries, I explore how bacterial density and confinement influence collective dynamics. The results contribute to a deeper understanding of bacterial activity in different settings, such as cloud droplets, with potential environmental applications.

Research Interests

  • Stochastic modeling and numerical simulations
  • Particle dynamics in confined systems
  • Soft matter physics and biophysics
